Dagbani Proverbs

The Dabgani-speaking ethnic group primarily resides in the northern part of Ghana, specifically in the Upper West Region. The Dabgani people have a rich cultural heritage deeply rooted in their agricultural practices, social structures, and spiritual beliefs.

  1. Proverb: Puu suyuli yi ŋmɛlim ka bɔ, di ku ŋmani kambɔŋa.
    • MEANING: No matter how big a hut on the farm becomes, it will not be as big as a granary.
    • APPLICATION: Don't compare yourself to someone who is greater than you. It may also mean we shouldn't compare ourselves to others who have something that we don't have. You may also have something that a person doesn't have.
  2. Proverb: Ziri yirigi pum, ka yɛlimaŋli deei wali.
    • MEANING: Lies produce flowers, but it is the truth that gets the fruits.
    • APPLICATION: The results of speaking the truth are always better than the results of telling lies. Or lies may carry you on your journey, but the one with truth will usually come and pass with more speed than yours.
  3. Proverb: Alizanda soli bili mɔri, wunti'zɔriba (wundabili) n-kani (n-naai)
    • MEANING: If heaven's path is grown over with grass, it means that there are no more good people around
    • APPLICATION: If good people do not speak out against bad things, they will continue to happen.
  4. Proverb: Din nam kamli, dina n-nam kamtoo.
    • MEANING: What has created the ordinary garden egg has also created the bitter one.
    • APPLICATION: Some people are good and some people are bad, but we are all descendants of the same ancestor. So we should learn to tolerate one another. This is often said to people during peace talks.
  5. Proverb: Gutulu ku sooŋa, n-tam wɔb'kura yɛla.
    • MEANING: An ungrateful man killed a hare and forgot about the elephant hunter.
    • APPLICATION: The elephant hunter had given him much meat and he didn't give anything in return. This is said to someone who you have helped a lot and he/she refuses you even a little help.
  6. Proverb: Jɛrigu ka yɛm shɛri dɔyim, amaa o lee mali yɛm ŋmari dɔyim
    • MEANING: The fool has no sense to unite a family, but he has sense to destroy a family.
    • APPLICATION: This is often said to someone who is using his talents to do evil to others instead of good; meanwhile evil has no reward but punishment from God.
  7. Proverb: Kay'shia mali waayo ku ŋmani piliŋ.
    • MEANING: No matter how sharp a split guinea stalk is, it can't be as sharp as a razor.
    • APPLICATION: You may have some strengths, talents, intelligence, wisdom or whatever, but don't think you can challenge everyone. This is said to someone who has been thinking he is the greatest in anything until he meets a bad day with someone greater than him/her.
  8. Proverb: Sataŋ kuyili baya ni nɔŋ'kpani
    • MEANING: The stone anvil doesn't care about the scorpion's spear
    • APPLICATION: I don't fear the bad deeds of an evil person; God is my protector. This is used in a situation where the speaker believes God has been his guard against his enemies' evil, machinations, ploy, lies etc.
  9. Proverb: Dahinshɛli beni ka jɛŋkuno ni ŋubi sima ka guui kɔhi n-ti jɛngbariga.
    • MEANING: There will be a day when the cat will eat groundnuts and have some leftover to sell for the benefit of the mouse.
    • APPLICATION: With hope there will be a day two archrivals will unite. Unity is always possible if there is hope. It may also mean that we should have hope that God will always provide our needs.
  10. Proverb: Baŋdi waayo ku baŋ nu' kpibili
    • MEANING: Even if you are an expert in understanding everything, you can't know what is inside a closed hand.
    • APPLICATION: No one knows tomorrow. You can't know exactly what is in the future. If there is an in-camera or closed door meeting of people you won't know the outcome until they tell you. If the gods have something for you, you won't know until it comes.

Nzema Proverbs

The Nzema-speaking ethnic group primarily resides in the Western Region of Ghana, particularly along the coastal areas. The Nzema people have a long and storied history, deeply intertwined with the coastal and fishing traditions of the region.

  1. Proverb: Wɔanzoa enrinli bile zo a ɛsoa egyɛne zo
    • MEANING:If you do not carry your load on black hair, you would carry it on grey hair
    • APPLICATION: Whoever fails to work hard at a youthful age would struggle at old age.
  2. Proverb: Nrenyia a kposa maa raalɛ di a
    • MEANING: It is a man who chews for a woman to swallow
    • APPLICATION:Men need to work hard to be able to feed their wives and children (family).
  3. Proverb: Ɛde ɛleka ko a ɛde wɔ debie zo
    • MEANING: If you sit at one place, you sit on something precious
    • APPLICATION: Whoever remains idle without working, stifles his/her progress and success
  4. Proverb: Gyima ɛsesebɛ ɛyɛlɛ ɛngu bɛ
    • MEANING: Difficult task does not kill people
    • APPLICATION: Hard work does not kill human being
  5. Proverb: Mɔɔ kɔ azule la anwo ɛmbɔ
    • MEANING: One who goes to fetch water does not experience body odour
    • APPLICATION: Whoever works hard, lives to benefit from it.
  6. Proverb: Menli dɔɔnwo debie ɛnle sonla ko deɛ
    • MEANING:Whatever belongs to many people is not an individual's assert
    • APPLICATION: One person cannot claim ownership of what belongs to a multitude of people
  7. Proverb: Aleɛ mɔɔ ɛkɛli bie la ɛwude-wuda ɔ bo
    • MEANING: You must rekindle the fire if you intend to join hands in eating the food
    • APPLICATION: One needs to work to help others achieve something for the mutual benefit
  8. Proverb: Mgbovonle ɛdanlɛ ku a ɔnnwu ye
    • MEANING: A lazy person refuses to recognise that his/her clothes are dirty
    • APPLICATION: People who do not want to work hard pretend as if they have no work to do
  9. Proverb: Saa wɔanvɛ a ɛnli
    • MEANING: If you do not strive hard, you do not eat also
    • APPLICATION: If you are lazy, you do not live a joyful life
  10. Proverb: Ɛsalɛ mɔɔ ɛnyɛ gyima la ɛnli aleɛ
    • MEANING: The hand that does not work does not eat also
    • APPLICATION: A lazy person goes hungry
