Dr Paul Farmer

Physician and Global public-health pioneer

A time line of Dr Farmer's life and achievements

  • 1959 - Born in North Adams, Massachusetts, U.S.
  • 1982 - Graduated from Duke University with a bachelor's degree in anthropology. Armed with a passion for understanding the intricate tapestry of human societies, Paul Farmer embarked on a journey to bring about change in the world of healthcare.
  • 1983 - Enrolled in Harvard Medical School, where he received his M.D. degree. With a stethoscope draped around his neck and a thirst for knowledge, Paul Farmer delved into the realm of medicine, eager to make a tangible difference in people's lives.
  • 1987 - Co-founded Partners In Health (PIH) along with Jim Yong Kim, Ophelia Dahl, Todd McCormack, and Thomas J. White. With a shared vision of providing healthcare to the most vulnerable, this dynamic team set out to revolutionize the way healthcare was delivered worldwide.
  • 1993 - Earned a Ph.D. in medical anthropology from Harvard University. Armed with a deep understanding of the cultural and social factors that shape health outcomes, Paul Farmer became a bridge between medicine and anthropology, striving to break down barriers that hindered effective healthcare delivery.
  • 1994 - Published the book "AIDS and Accusation: Haiti and the Geography of Blame," unraveling the complex web of social stigmas surrounding HIV in Haiti. Paul Farmer's work shed light on the underlying causes of the epidemic, challenging the world to confront not just the disease but also the systemic issues that perpetuated its spread.
  • 1996 - Expanded Partners In Health's operations to Rwanda, where they faced the arduous task of providing healthcare in the aftermath of a devastating genocide. Undeterred by the scars of conflict, Paul Farmer and his team embarked on a mission to heal both physical and emotional wounds, bringing hope to a nation grappling with profound loss.
  • 2001 - Received a MacArthur Fellowship, earning him the nickname "the man who won a 'genius grant' to fight infectious diseases." With newfound resources and recognition, Paul Farmer blazed a trail in delivering innovative healthcare solutions to underserved communities, challenging the notion that limited resources should dictate health outcomes.
  • 2003 - Helped establish the Global Fund to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis, and Malaria, becoming a catalyst for global collaboration in combating these deadly diseases. In the face of bureaucratic hurdles and political resistance, Paul Farmer stood as a fierce advocate, rallying nations to unite in the fight for global health equity.
  • 2009 - Appointed as the United Nations Deputy Special Envoy to Haiti, Paul Farmer faced the daunting task of rebuilding a shattered healthcare system in the aftermath of a catastrophic earthquake. Amidst the rubble and chaos, he championed resilience, transforming tragedy into an opportunity to reshape the future of healthcare in Haiti.
  • 2012 - Published the book "To Repair the World: Paul Farmer Speaks to the Next Generation," a beacon of inspiration for those seeking to make a difference. With words that echoed with passion and wisdom, Paul Farmer empowered the next generation to rise up, challenging them to question the status quo and forge a path toward health justice.
  • 2014 - Named the Kolokotrones University Professor and Chair of the Department of Global Health and Social Medicine at Harvard Medical School. In the hallowed halls of academia, Paul Farmer waged a quiet revolution, breaking down silos and urging fellow scholars to embrace a holistic approach to healthcare that encompassed both the biological and social determinants of well-being.
  • 2017 - Elected to the National Academy of Medicine, Paul Farmer's induction into this prestigious circle of luminaries marked a pinnacle of recognition for his tireless efforts. Armed with a seat at the table, he continued to challenge the establishment, ensuring that the voices of the marginalized were heard and their health needs addressed.
  • 2018 - Received the Berggruen Prize for Philosophy and Culture, a testament to his unwavering dedication to social justice. Standing before an audience of scholars and visionaries, Paul Farmer reminded the world that the pursuit of health equity was not just a noble cause but a moral imperative, urging society to dismantle the barriers that perpetuate inequality.
  • 2022 - Died in Butaro, Rwanda.
